RadioShack TRS-80 Model 4 Gate-Array Repair
Introduction

THE KEYBOARD
(THIS REPAIR WAS LIKE 4-5 YEARS AGO)
When I first fired the computer up, and put in a disk, there was barely any input working from the computer. However when there was, multiple keys would be pressed multiple times. For example, pressing 9 would lead to something like ‘@@$ppp'.

This was because the ‘Gate Array' edition of the computer contained cheaper parts. Instead of a good mechanical keyboard, this TRS-80 was cursed with a membrane keyboard. Not just any membrane keyboard! The membrane had painted circuits that conducted electricity when you pushed down on a key, which contained a metal spring. The paint sucks, the springs can misalign and fall out easily, and good god the whole thing sucks. And cleaning the board? It did nothing!
REPLACING THE KEYBOARD
(STILL A 5 YEAR OLD REPAIR.)
So I decided to replace the keyboard. The Non-Gate-Array edition had a satisfying mechanical keyboard, but it was over $200 on eBay, so I had to find a better option.
Luckily for me, Plaid Vest Software created a solution known as the NEWKEY/80, which replaced the crummy keyboard with a USB port that you could use instead.
So, I installed the component. The instructions were easy, and made sense. It taps into the power for the floppy drives and just drops in from the keyboard power cable.
That should be the end of it, but I need to say really quick that I was very stupid when I was first doing these repairs. I wanted the keyboard to feel authentic and fit inside the case. So, I cut the pegs that held the original keyboard in place. It was a poor fix, the keyboard still didn't fit, and now an original keyboard would struggle to stay inside. Note to self, DON'T DO DESTRUCTIVE REPAIRS. However from this I have since learned my lesson.
